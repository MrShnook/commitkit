# CommitKit — SEO Content Strategy

*Created: 2026-03-02*

## Strategy Overview

CommitKit's content strategy targets parents of competitive youth athletes (Class of 2027-2030) who are actively searching for college recruiting guidance. We own the **"family-side recruiting management"** angle — no competitor occupies this space.

### Content Pillars

1. **Recruiting Process Guides** — "How to" content that ranks for informational queries
2. **NCSA / Competitor Alternatives** — Capture frustrated families switching away
3. **NCAA Rules & Timelines** — Evergreen reference content updated annually
4. **Sport-Specific Guides** — Start with soccer (our strength), expand to basketball, baseball, volleyball
5. **Parent Empowerment** — Advice on the family dynamic, letting the athlete lead, managing stress

### SEO Positioning

**Primary angle:** We're the practical, parent-to-parent resource. Not a recruiting agency, not a sales pitch. Real guidance from families who've been through it.

**Content differentiation from competitors:**
- NCSA's blog: Sales-focused, drives toward $4K packages
- Anytime Soccer: Good content but no product — pure affiliate
- SportsRecruits: Coach-facing, not parent-facing
- Us: Parent-facing, tool-integrated, anti-NCSA positioning

## Initial Blog Posts (5 Posts, Priority Order)

### Post 1: "The Complete College Soccer Recruiting Timeline (2026-2027)"
**Target keywords:** college soccer recruiting timeline, when to start recruiting for soccer, NCAA soccer recruiting calendar
**Search intent:** Informational — parents wanting a clear roadmap
**Word count:** 2,000-2,500
**Format:** Month-by-month timeline (freshman through senior year) with actionable checklists
**Hook:** "Most families start too late. Here's exactly when to do what — month by month."
**CTA:** Free recruiting timeline tracker (waitlist signup)
**AI-SEO elements:**
- Table: Grade-by-grade recruiting milestones
- FAQ: 5-7 common timeline questions
- Data points: NCAA contact dates (June 15), signing periods, dead periods
- Expert framing: "As parents of an MLS Next player currently navigating this..."
**Internal links:** → Post 2 (NCSA), Post 3 (email templates), Post 5 (checklist)
**Estimated difficulty:** Medium (competing with NCSA, Anytime Soccer, NCSA calendar pages)

### Post 2: "NCSA Alternatives: What Smart Families Use Instead (2026 Guide)"
**Target keywords:** NCSA alternatives, is NCSA worth it, NCSA review, NCSA scam, NCSA competitors
**Search intent:** Commercial — families actively evaluating options
**Word count:** 2,500-3,000
**Format:** Comparison article with honest pros/cons of each option
**Hook:** "NCSA charges $1,000-4,000 for a service many coaches call unnecessary. Here's what actually works."
**Competitors to cover:**
- NCSA (detailed review with BBB data)
- SportsRecruits (pros/cons)
- FieldLevel (pros/cons)
- CaptainU (pros/cons)
- ProductiveRecruit (pros/cons)
- DIY (Google Sheets approach)
- CommitKit (positioned as the organized DIY upgrade)
**CTA:** "Try CommitKit free — track 3 schools, no credit card"
**AI-SEO elements:**
- Comparison table: Features × competitors
- Pricing table: Annual cost breakdown
- FAQ: "Is NCSA a scam?", "What does NCSA actually do?", "Can I recruit without NCSA?"
- BBB complaint statistics (sourced)
- Reddit/community sentiment summary
**Internal links:** → Post 1 (timeline), Post 4 (how coaches recruit)
**Estimated difficulty:** Medium-high (NCSA owns their own brand terms, but discontent creates opportunity)

### Post 3: "College Soccer Coach Email Templates: What to Write (With Examples)"
**Target keywords:** college soccer recruiting email template, how to email college soccer coaches, recruiting email examples
**Search intent:** Informational/transactional — families ready to take action
**Word count:** 1,800-2,200
**Format:** 6-8 copy-paste email templates organized by stage
**Hook:** "Your first email to a college coach is your first impression. Here's exactly what to write."
**Templates to include:**
1. Initial introduction email
2. Post-showcase/camp follow-up
3. Monthly update email
4. After a campus visit
5. Expressing strong interest
6. Responding to coach interest
7. Thank you / after commitment
**CTA:** "CommitKit tracks which coaches you've emailed and reminds you when to follow up"
**AI-SEO elements:**
- Template text blocks (copy-paste ready)
- Subject line suggestions table
- "Do" and "Don't" comparison table
- FAQ: "Should parents or athletes send emails?", "How often to follow up?"
**Internal links:** → Post 1 (timeline for when to send each), Post 2 (alternatives that help)
**Estimated difficulty:** Low-medium

### Post 4: "How College Soccer Coaches Actually Recruit (From the Coach's Perspective)"
**Target keywords:** how do college soccer coaches recruit, college soccer recruiting process coaches, what coaches look for
**Search intent:** Informational — parents wanting to understand the other side
**Word count:** 2,000-2,500
**Format:** Narrative article with coach quotes and data
**Hook:** "You're sending emails to coaches. But do you know what they're looking for?"
**Key angles:**
- How coaches build their recruiting boards
- What they look for in a first email
- Red flags that eliminate recruits (hovering parents, unrealistic expectations)
- Transfer portal impact on roster spots (post-2025 rules)
- The Pat Fitzgerald quote about evaluating parents
**CTA:** "CommitKit helps your family look organized and professional"
**AI-SEO elements:**
- Coach perspective quotes (sourced from interviews, articles)
- Table: "What coaches evaluate" (Athletic ability, Academics, Character, Family dynamic)
- NCAA roster cap data (28-player limit, July 2025)
**Internal links:** → Post 3 (email templates), Post 1 (timeline), Post 5 (checklist)
**Estimated difficulty:** Medium

### Post 5: "The College Soccer Recruiting Checklist: Every Step from Freshman to Signing Day"
**Target keywords:** college soccer recruiting checklist, college recruiting checklist for parents, soccer recruiting steps

## Technical SEO Requirements

- Route: /blog/[slug] in Next.js app router
- MDX or markdown content with frontmatter
- Auto-generated sitemap.xml + robots.txt
- Canonical URLs, Open Graph + Twitter Card meta
- Schema markup: Article, FAQ, HowTo
- Last updated dates (freshness signal)
- Self-hosted fonts (done ✅)
- Static generation for blog posts
