# CommitKit — Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs)

*Research completed: 2026-03-01*

## ICP #1: The "Organized Soccer Dad/Mom" (PRIMARY)

### Demographics
- **Age:** 40-55
- **Income:** $80K-200K household
- **Location:** Suburban USA, clustered around major club soccer markets (TX, CA, FL, GA, NC, AZ, CO, NJ, NY, IL)
- **Education:** College-educated (likely values education for their kids)

### Profile
- Parent of a competitive youth soccer player (ages 14-18)
- Player is on MLS Next, ECNL, GA, or strong high school varsity team
- Currently managing recruitment with spreadsheets + email + memory
- Has attended 3-15+ ID camps and showcases
- Has a target school list (even if informal) of 10-30+ programs
- Already spending $5K-20K/year on the recruitment process

### Pain Points
1. **Information overload:** Dozens of schools, coaches, timelines, requirements — no central place to track it all
2. **Follow-up fatigue:** Sent emails to 20 coaches, lost track of who responded and when to follow up
3. **Eligibility anxiety:** NCAA core course requirements, test scores, registration deadlines — am I on track?
4. **Camp/showcase chaos:** Which camps are worth it? Which coaches will be there? Which are predatory?
5. **Emotional rollercoaster:** Coach interest → silence → hope → rejection cycles with no visibility

### Buying Triggers
- Just started the recruitment process (sophomore year)
- Spreadsheet hit 50+ rows and became unmanageable
- Missed a follow-up with a coach and lost an opportunity
- Friend's kid committed and they realize they're behind
- June 15 approaching (D1/D2 contact date)

### Where They Hang Out
- Facebook groups: "MLS Next Parents," "ECNL Parents," sport-specific recruiting groups
- Reddit: r/bootroom, r/CollegeSoccer
- Sideline conversations at games and tournaments
- Club parent meetings and WhatsApp groups
- YouTube: recruiting advice channels

### Willingness to Pay
**HIGH.** Already spending $10K+/year. A $19/mo tool is <2% of annual recruitment spend. If it saves one missed follow-up or one wasted camp trip, it pays for itself instantly.

### Key Quote Archetype
> "I have a Google Sheet with 25 schools, but I can't remember which coaches I emailed last week. My wife has a different list in her Notes app. We're going to miss something important."

## ICP #2: The "Club Director / DOC" (SECONDARY — Future)

### Demographics
- **Role:** Director of Coaching, Club Administrator, or Head Coach at a competitive club
- **Club Size:** 50-500 families
- **Leagues:** MLS Next, ECNL, GA, or strong state leagues

### Profile
- Responsible for guiding families through the recruitment process
- Currently uses generic email templates and one-off conversations
- Wants to provide value to families as a retention/differentiation tool
- May already use ProductiveRecruit or similar B2B tool

### Pain Points
1. **Repeated questions:** Every parent asks the same recruitment questions
2. **No scalable guidance:** Can't personally hand-hold 200 families through the process
3. **Retention pressure:** Families leave clubs that don't support the recruitment journey
4. **Coach relationships:** Knows college coaches but no systematic way to connect families

### Buying Trigger
- Losing families to clubs that offer "college placement" programs
- ProductiveRecruit/NCSA sales reps targeting their club

### Willingness to Pay
**MODERATE.** Would pay $50-200/mo for a club-level license if it includes family-facing tools. Or refer families to a self-serve tool (affiliate model).

### Why SECONDARY
This is the ProductiveRecruit market. We don't compete here first — we win families directly (B2C), then potentially add a B2B layer (club dashboard) once we have traction.

## ICP #3: The "Multi-Sport Family" (EXPANSION)

### Profile
- Child plays multiple sports competitively
- May be recruited for soccer AND track, or soccer AND lacrosse
- Needs to track recruitment across multiple sports simultaneously
- Even more overwhelmed than single-sport families

### Why EXPANSION
The core product is soccer-first. Multi-sport support is a natural upsell once the soccer product is mature. Same CRM, different sport metadata.
