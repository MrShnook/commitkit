# CommitKit — Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-03-01*

## Product Overview

**One-liner:** The recruiting command center for sports families.

**What it does:** CommitKit helps families of competitive youth athletes manage the multi-year college recruiting process. Track schools on a Kanban pipeline, manage coach outreach and follow-ups, monitor eligibility timelines, and organize showcases — all in one place. Think "HubSpot for soccer families."

**Product category:** College recruiting management / Sports recruitment tools

**Product type:** SaaS (web app)

**Business model:** Freemium → paid subscription
- Free: Track up to 3 schools (experience the value)
- Pro: $19/mo or $149/year — unlimited schools, coach CRM, eligibility tracking, email templates
- Premium (future): $29/mo or $229/year — analytics, benchmarking, multi-sport

## Target Audience

**Target user:** Parents of competitive youth athletes (ages 14-18) actively pursuing college recruitment
**Secondary user:** The student-athlete (needs to lead communication with coaches)

**Primary use case:** Managing the chaotic, multi-year college recruiting process — tracking dozens of schools, hundreds of coach emails, scholarship deadlines, and showcase events.

**Jobs to be done:**
- "Help me keep track of which coaches I've contacted and who responded"
- "Help me not miss important NCAA deadlines"
- "Help me organize which camps and showcases are worth attending"
- "Help me stop feeling overwhelmed by this process"

**Use cases:**
- Building and managing a target school list (Dream / Target / Safety)
- Tracking coach outreach and follow-up timing
- Monitoring NCAA/NAIA/JUCO eligibility requirements
- Planning showcase attendance based on which coaches will be there
- Coordinating between both parents (shared view of recruitment progress)

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** The college recruiting process is a 2-3 year, high-stakes journey with zero structure. Families manage it with spreadsheets, email inboxes, and memory — then miss follow-ups, deadlines, and opportunities.

**Why current solutions fall short:**
- NCSA ($1K-4K/yr): Predatory pricing, vanishing support, coaches call it a scam
- SportsRecruits: Profile platform, not a workflow tool — no CRM, no timelines
- FieldLevel: Coach-driven, family is passive — you wait and hope
- CaptainU: Basic messaging, no process management
- Spreadsheets: No follow-up reminders, no shared access, no guidance

**What it costs them:**
- Time: 5-10 hrs/week tracking manually during peak recruiting season
- Money: $5K-20K/year wasted on camps/showcases without strategy
- Opportunities: Missed follow-ups = lost coaching interest (silent rejection)
- Relationships: Family stress — parents and kids fighting over "did you email that coach?"

**Emotional tension:** Fear of their kid missing out. Guilt about not doing enough. Anxiety about spending thousands and getting nothing. Confusion about what to do next.

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct:** NCSA — falls short because predatory pricing ($1K-4K/yr), aggressive upsells, coaches warn families away. BBB complaint factory.

**Direct:** SportsRecruits, FieldLevel, CaptainU — fall short because they're profile/marketplace platforms. Families CREATE profiles and WAIT. No active process management.

**Secondary:** ProductiveRecruit — closest competitor, has CRM features, but targets clubs/organizations, not individual families. B2B, not B2C.

**Indirect:** Google Sheets / DIY — falls short because no follow-up reminders, no eligibility tracking, no templates, no shared access, no guidance on WHAT to do.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
- ONLY tool built for the family's workflow (not coach-side, not marketplace)
- Kanban pipeline for schools (Dream → Target → Safety → Contacted → Committed)
- Coach outreach CRM with follow-up reminders and email templates
- Eligibility timeline with NCAA/NAIA/JUCO deadline tracking
- 10-50x cheaper than NCSA ($19/mo vs. $100-350/mo)
- Cancel anytime, transparent pricing, no sales calls

**How we do it differently:** We don't connect you with coaches (marketplace model) or host your profile (platform model). We give you the TOOLS to run your own recruiting process — organized, confident, and on schedule.

**Why that's better:** Families who run their own process get better results than families who pay NCSA to do it. Coaches respect students who reach out directly and follow up professionally. CommitKit makes that easy.

**Why customers choose us:** "I don't need someone to sell me access to coaches. I need help managing the 47 schools on my list."

## Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We already have a spreadsheet" | A spreadsheet doesn't remind you to follow up, track which coaches opened your email, or warn you about NCAA deadlines. CommitKit does all of that — and syncs between both parents. |
| "NCSA already does this" | NCSA sells access to a marketplace. CommitKit gives you tools to manage YOUR process. Coaches themselves say NCSA "is a scam" — we cost $19/mo, not $4,000. |
| "My kid's club handles recruiting" | Some clubs are great at it. Most aren't. And even the best clubs can't give your family personalized tracking of 30+ schools. CommitKit complements your club's guidance. |

**Anti-persona:**
- Families who want someone else to do everything (they want a concierge, not a tool)
- Recreational players with no college sports ambitions
- Coaches looking for a scouting tool

## Switching Dynamics

**Push:** Spreadsheet has 50+ rows and is unmanageable. Missed a follow-up. NCSA charged $4K and did nothing. Anxiety about deadlines.

**Pull:** See everything in one place. Never miss a follow-up. Know exactly what to do next. Affordable.

**Habit:** "My spreadsheet works fine" (until it doesn't). Inertia — already paid NCSA so might as well stick.

**Anxiety:** "Will my data be safe?" "Is this just another company trying to profit from desperate parents?" "What if I'm paying for something I could do for free?"

**Words to use:** commit, recruiting journey, college-bound, process, organized, on track, follow up, outreach, NLI, verbal commit, target list, safety school, dream school

**Words to avoid:** scam, hustle, guaranteed, "get recruited" (implies passivity), student-athlete (overused), synergy, leverage

**Glossary:**
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Verbal commit | Unofficial agreement between athlete and school (non-binding) |
| NLI | National Letter of Intent — binding commitment to a school |
| D1/D2/D3 | NCAA Division levels (D1 highest) |
| NAIA | Smaller college athletic association |
| JUCO | Junior college (2-year) |
| Showcase/ID camp | Events where athletes perform for college coaches |
| June 15 | Date D1/D2 coaches can first directly contact rising juniors |
| Contact period | When coaches can actively reach out to recruits |
